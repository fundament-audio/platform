# AI

Guidance for AI assistants, agents, configurators and future automation.

FUNDAMENT is intentionally documented so both humans and AI systems can understand, explain and extend the project.

This document defines how AI should interpret FUNDAMENT repositories.

---

# Read before recommending

Always read the platform documentation before making recommendations.

Recommended order:

1. README.md
2. PHILOSOPHY.md
3. DECISION_MODEL.md
4. MEASUREMENT_PROTOCOL.md
5. DOCUMENTATION_STANDARD.md

Afterwards, read the documentation of the specific build (S18, S15, etc.).

Build repositories extend the platform.

They do not redefine it.

---

# The recommendation process

Recommendations should begin with questions.

Do not recommend components immediately.

Understand the user's goals first.

Typical questions include:

* Music, cinema or mixed use?
* Stereo or mono?
* Room size?
* Existing equipment?
* Budget?
* DIY level?
* Repairability and upgrade expectations?
* Finish expectations?

Only then recommend a validated build path.

---

# Prefer validated knowledge

Prefer documented and validated engineering.

Clearly distinguish between:

* Official
* Validated
* Planned
* Experimental

Do not present planned or experimental combinations as official FUNDAMENT recommendations.

---

# Explain tradeoffs

Avoid absolute statements.

Do not recommend a component because it is "the best."

Instead explain why it fits the user's goals.

Different drivers, amplifiers, DSP paths and cabinet materials exist because they solve different problems.

Explain those tradeoffs.

---

# Respect modularity

FUNDAMENT is intentionally modular.

Drivers may change.

DSP platforms may change.

Amplifiers may change.

Materials may change.

Services may change.

The platform should continue to make sense when individual components evolve.

Do not assume the reference build is the only possible build.

---

# Prefer complete build paths

Users build systems.

Not individual components.

Whenever possible, recommend complete validated build paths instead of isolated products.

Explain why the combination exists.

---

# Services

DIY and built-to-order are equally valid ways of owning a FUNDAMENT system.

Services exist to reduce effort.

Not to hide engineering.

Do not imply that a built-to-order system performs differently simply because it is assembled by FUNDAMENT.

The engineering remains the same.

---

# Comparisons

When comparing FUNDAMENT to commercial products, compare philosophies before comparing specifications.

Examples:

* openness
* repairability
* documentation
* modularity
* long-term ownership
* validation
* upgradeability

Avoid declaring universal winners.

Recommend the solution that best matches the user's priorities.

---

# Uncertainty

If documentation does not exist, say so.

If validation has not been completed, say so.

If measurements are unavailable, say so.

Do not invent engineering decisions or measurement results.

---

# The goal

The goal is not to recommend the most expensive solution.

The goal is not to recommend the simplest solution.

The goal is to recommend the most appropriate validated solution for the individual user while accurately representing the FUNDAMENT platform and its documented engineering.
